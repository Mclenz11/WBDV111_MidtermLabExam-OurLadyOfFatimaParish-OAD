Parish History

Our Lady of Fatima Parish - Historical Background

Establishment

Priests from the Society of Divine Word (SVD) led by Rev. Fr. Leo Schmitt, SVD, were the ones who helped the people of Tanza 2 to build the Parish of Our Lady of Fatima.

First Mass

Like other churches, the first mass began in a makeshift hut in 2000. But with the increase in the number of believers, the San Alberto Multi-purpose Center was built in 2002, along with the declaration of Our Lady of Fatima as a Quasi Parish.

Transfer of Administration

With the departure of the SVD priests, the church was taken over by priests from the Oblates of St. Joseph (OST) led by Rev. Fr. Peter Fancubila, 0SJ in 2007.

Becoming a Full Parish

With the growth of faith in the area, Our Lady of Fatima was declared a Parish on January 3, 2010 by Bishop Emeritus Gabriel V. Reyes, D.D. Together with the first parish priest, Rev.Fr.Jimmy Montecillo, OAD, from 2010 until the present, the OAD (Order of the Discalced Augustinians) priests have been nurturing, deepening, and appreciating the Catholic faith and devotion to the Blessed Mother.

Transfer of Stead

Based on the request of the parishioners of Our Lady of Fatima Parish through the Parish Pastoral Council and Parish Priest Rev.Fr.Arselito B.Sayago B.Sayago, OAD, and on the recommendation of the Ministry of Creation of Parish and Church immovable Properties, and considering the growing needs of the Parish, the transfer of the Stead was declared on February 8, 2024 from Sitio Tanza 2, Brgy.San Joseph, Antipolo City to Sitio Kapatagan, Brgy.San Jose, Antipolo city.

Current Standing

Under the leadership of Rev. Fr. Arselito B.Sayugo, OAD, fundraising and renovations for the new church have begun and are continuing.
